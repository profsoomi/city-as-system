Aesthetics Department

All islands follow the general dress codes and architecture rules, but also each have their own regulations.

Palace Island

❖ Buildings must correspond and conform to the overall shape of Clarence.

❖ Structures must incorporate green, yellow, grey or a combination of all three.

❖ Potential development on green spaces must be made on Palace Island.

Mushroom Island

❖ Development must be related to the maintenance or improvement of the garden.

❖ Structures must incorporate two (2) of the following: yellow, green, purple, blue, pink or burgundy.

The Sand Bars

❖ Development on the shores must not inconvenience or disturb the mermaids in any way.

❖ Proposals to develop the sand bars are subject to a lengthy approval process due to the limited amount of space available.

The Farm

❖ Development outside of the central township is subject to input from farmers and farm animals.

❖ Development inside certain regions of The Farm must be subject to the same color code as the surrounding area (ex. Blue land, blue buildings).

Trash Island

❖ Development close the processing center must be related to trash processing.

❖ Proposals for developing tourist areas in the northern and southern regions of the island will be prioritized.

Grey Island

❖ Any future development made within certain neighborhoods on Grey Island must correspond to the surrounding color (Ex. Dark grey neighborhood, dark grey building).

❖ Development shall not exceed the the boundaries of each neighborhood, or else it will disrupt the overall cohesiveness of the island.

  • Created out of a need for a more unified aesthetic, our department’s design system is the standard across the city.
  • We routinely meet with the Monarchy to discuss potential changes or feedback on our current system.
  • Our design system includes assets such as our logo, primary/secondary colors and typography.
  • Other departments follow a looser interpretation of our design system, which can be viewed by clicking on the image of the design system.

Regulations and Guidelines

  • Failure to follow style guidelines is punishable by death.
  • Shorts are not allowed.
  • Civilians are required to wear mushroom hats.
  • All prints need to incorporate mushrooms.
  • Mushroom-based fabric.
  • All buttons/zipper need to be mushroom shaped.

Regulations and Guidelines

  • Mushroom-shaped houses only.
  • No concrete or brick allowed.
  • Height of buildings are restricted by size of monarchy quarters.
  • All architectural accents must feature mushroom imagery.
  • Furniture and upholstery must be mushroom shaped as well.
